# World Reference

Quick reference for world context when writing level design lore. For full canon details, see [politicalHistory.md](documentation/lore/politicalHistory.md).

## Timeline Overview

| Era               | Years     | Key Characteristics                                     |
| ----------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Imperial Collapse | 1050-1100 | Old empire falling, chaos, power vacuums                |
| Transition Period | 1100-1250 | New order emerging, Confederation forming               |
| Prosperous Years  | 1250-1350 | Trade expansion, relative stability, wealth growth      |
| Current Crisis    | 1350-1420 | Tensions rising, factions competing, resources strained |

**Most content occurs during Current Crisis (1350-1420).**

## Major Factions

### Oathkeepers

- **Role**: Traditional military order, defenders of old values
- **Voice**: Formal, duty-bound, honor-focused, suspicious of change
- **Concerns**: Military readiness, border security, traditional hierarchy
- **Speech patterns**: "By my oath...", references to duty and honor, military terminology
- **Tensions with**: Mercantile Houses (profit over duty), Centralists (changing traditions)

### Mercantile Houses

- **Role**: Trade guilds and commercial interests
- **Voice**: Pragmatic, diplomatic, profit-minded, calculating
- **Concerns**: Trade routes, market stability, regulatory burden
- **Speech patterns**: Transactional language, cost-benefit framing, "investment", "returns"
- **Tensions with**: Oathkeepers (expensive military), Local Lords (toll disputes)

### Tributary Board

- **Role**: Tax collection and administrative bureaucracy
- **Voice**: Procedural, neutral, by-the-book, detached
- **Concerns**: Revenue collection, record keeping, jurisdictional clarity
- **Speech patterns**: Forms and procedures, "according to regulation", passive voice
- **Tensions with**: Everyone (they collect taxes from all)

### Velistran Partners

- **Role**: Maritime trade interests from city-state of Velistra
- **Voice**: Cosmopolitan, trade-focused, maritime metaphors, worldly
- **Concerns**: Sea trade, port access, foreign relations
- **Speech patterns**: Sailing metaphors ("smooth sailing", "choppy waters"), foreign loanwords
- **Tensions with**: Confederation generally (outsider status), protectionist factions

### Local Lords

- **Role**: Regional nobility with traditional territorial rights
- **Voice**: Provincial, territorial, practical, locally-focused
- **Concerns**: Local autonomy, traditional privileges, regional interests
- **Speech patterns**: Local idioms, references to land and family, "my people"
- **Tensions with**: Centralists (loss of autonomy), Tributary Board (tax demands)

## Key Locations

### Greyfen

- **Type**: Major Confederation settlement
- **Character**: Administrative center, garrison town, commercial hub
- **Tone**: Busy, bureaucratic, mix of military and merchant
- **Details**: Stone walls, market squares, garrison quarters, warehouses

### Velistra

- **Type**: Maritime city-state
- **Character**: Independent trading power, cosmopolitan, wealthy
- **Tone**: Foreign, mercantile, seafaring culture
- **Details**: Harbors, merchant palaces, foreign quarters, shipyards

### Confederation Heartland

- **Type**: Central territories
- **Character**: Agricultural, traditional, relatively stable
- **Tone**: Pastoral, conservative, suspicious of outsiders

### Borderlands

- **Type**: Frontier regions
- **Character**: Contested, rough, independent-minded
- **Tone**: Tense, practical, every person for themselves

## Tone Guidelines

### DO Write

- Political tensions grounded in economics and power
- Personal stakes over world-ending threats
- Moral ambiguity (no clear heroes/villains)
- Consequences that feel real and lasting
- Characters with practical concerns (money, family, survival)

### DON'T Write

- Cosmic evil or apocalyptic threats
- Chosen one narratives
- Clear good vs evil divisions
- Fantasy racism as world-building shortcut
- Exposition dumps disguised as dialogue

## Period-Appropriate Phrases

### Greetings/Acknowledgments

- "Well met" / "Fair meeting"
- "By your leave"
- "As you say"
- "Aye" / "Nay"

### Expressions

- "Fortune's favor" (good luck)
- "A fool's bargain"
- "Mark my words"
- "By the old roads" (oath, mild)

### Avoid (Modern/Anachronistic)

- "Okay" / "Sure" / "Yeah"
- "No problem" / "You got it"
- "Awesome" / "Cool"
- Military jargon post-1800s ("roger", "copy that", "affirmative")
- Business jargon ("synergy", "leverage", "optimize")

## Combat Terminology

### Movement

- "Advance" / "Fall back" / "Hold position"
- "Flanking" / "Pincer" / "Encircle"
- "Form up" / "Disperse" / "Rally"

### Combat

- "Engage" / "Press the attack" / "Disengage"
- "Volley" / "Loose" (arrows)
- "Charge" / "Brace"

### Status

- "Wounded" / "Bloodied" / "Down"
- "Holding" / "Hard pressed" / "Overrun"
- "Clear" / "Secure" / "Contested"

## Magic in the World

Magic exists but is:

- **Rare**: Most people never see real magic
- **Practical**: Used for specific purposes, not flashy displays
- **Costly**: Has real costs (materials, exhaustion, consequences)
- **Grounded**: Follows rules, not arbitrary wonder

When writing magic:

- Treat it like a tool or skill, not a miracle
- Show practical limitations
- Characters react like it's unusual but known to exist
- Avoid "chosen one" magical bloodlines
